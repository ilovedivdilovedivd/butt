Could consuming alcohol within 24 hours of lip injections dissolve filler & not achieve full results?
Should not be a problem
Sep 21, 2023
While you are correct that consuming alcohol can increase swelling, there is no evidence that this will “dissolve” your filler. However, I would be cautious in getting lip filler before an event, as swelling following injections can be significant and asymmetric. I often tell my patients not to get filler within two weeks of an event so that any swelling can subside and we can make any minor adjustments prior to that.

Should I wait on a reduction if my breasts are still growing?
Absolutely
May 2, 2023
It is best to wait until your breasts have reached a stable size for at least 6 months prior to pursuing a breast reduction. This ensures that we are able to reduce the breasts to the appropriate size with minimal risk of the affected tissue having additional hypertrophy, or growth, following the removal.
High BMI. Will I be able to get surgery without losing a bunch of weight?
Not recommended
Apr 27, 2023
In general, a BMI of below 35 is recommended for body contouring surgery of any kind. All complications, but especially VTE (venous thromboembolic, or blood clots) events, increase over a BMI of 35. There are some surgeons who are comfortable operating up to a BMI of 40, but this is not generally supported by the literature. Make sure you are seeking surgery from a surgeon specifically board certified in plastic surgery to make sure all safety considerations are taken into account.

Breast implant revision. When should adderall be stopped pre-surgery?
One Week
Apr 27, 2023
In general, medical grade stimulants such as Adderall should be stopped one week prior to surgery due to anesthetic concerns. Caffeine is OK to continue until the day before surgery. These medications can be restarted the day after surgery as they do not generally affect wound healing or other surgical concerns.
Could my platysmaplasty be the cause of my breathing problems / tongue position?
Not Likely
Apr 24, 2023
There should be no physiologic reason that the platysmaplasty should be causing any issues with the tongue. There are some patients that endorse a temporary feeling of tightness of the neck and airway following a neck lift, but this generally subsides over time. I would recommend consulting with an otolaryngologist (ENT) to ensure there are no other physiologic abnormalities causing your symptoms.
Is risk of blood clots higher if you have your period before or after surgery?
No Correlation
Apr 24, 2023
There is no correlation between the timing of menses and the risk of a VTE (venous thromboembolic) event. It would be safe to proceed with surgery as planned.
