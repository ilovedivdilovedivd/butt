Calculating the Cost for a fat transfer to buttocks

More so than in any other procedure, time is the greatest factor in the cost of a Brazilian Butt Lift. Of course, a surgeon’s experience and skill will also determine the rate for this procedure, but the time needed due to the complexity and amount of work on a given patient is the most significant variable.

Time is influenced by: a patient’s initial body structure, number of areas of liposuction, harvesting technique, and transfer technique. A patient’s initial body structure (bone and muscle structure, amount and distribution of fat) greatly influences the amount of work necessary to obtain a given result.

Someone who is heavier may have a more difficult time obtaining the shape or proportions they want. Someone who is thinner is going to require more areas to obtain fat for transfer.

And will need a skilled and experienced surgeon to harvest the fat while ensuring that the areas that have been liposuctioned still look smooth, natural and proportionate. In order to get the size and shape you desire, extra care and time for this procedure is needed to properly process the harvested fat and to properly perform the transfer.

Otherwise, the percentage of fat survival will be low. If you receive a quote that seems too good to be true, or if you see a low advertised price for a fat transfer to buttocks, there’s a good chance that you will be short-changed somewhere along your procedure. I have seen many patients who get sucked in by these ploys and then need correction of poorly performed fat transfers.

Also, it is important to remember that the way a patient takes care of themselves after surgery greatly influences the final results. I take a lot of time in educating my patients prior to surgery and maintaining close follow-up care to optimize the results. Following their post-op instructions results in a higher percentage of fat survival for the patient. On average, fat transfer to buttocks without liposuction (assuming separate costs for liposuction or using previously harvested fat) would range from $3000-$6000. (Gabriel Chiu, DO, Beverly Hills Plastic Surgeon)

Brazilian Butt Lift Cost. Connecticut

The brazilian butt lift or buttock augmentation has steadily gaining popularity. As new and improved techniques are available it is now more affordable and with less downtime than patients previously experienced. Using fat from one area (that you may not want) and moving it to an area you do want it (usually the buttock) is without a doubt a big draw to this procedure.

However, this procedure takes a lot of skill and practice. Harvesting the fat for re-injection has to be done this the right equipment and care. Here is some basic information regarding the procedure:

  • Duration of surgery for a Buttock Augmentation: 1.5 – 3 hours depending on the amount of fat harvested and re-injected
  • Harvest Technique: Use of Power Assisted Liposuction to harvest fat with speed. and use of LipoFilter™ which is a large-volume (3-liter), dual-chamber, fat harvesting and separation system, designed to maintain high cell viability.
  • Anesthesia: General by a board certified anesthesiologist in an accredited surgical facility or hospital
  • Recovery after a Buttock Augmentation:1-2 hours in the Recovery room3 days of management of surgical discomfort with pain meds and muscle relaxant
  • 10-14 days: resume your activitiesof daily living
  • 4-6 weeks: Full exercise
  • Cost of a Buttock Augmentation: $5000-$7000 depending on the amount of time needed in the OR and anesthesia. (Alfred Sofer, MD, FACS, Fairfield Plastic Surgeon)
