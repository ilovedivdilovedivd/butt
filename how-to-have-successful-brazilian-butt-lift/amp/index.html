Brazilian butt lift definition

A brazilian butt lift is a technique where the buttocks are reshaped and augmented with the use of your own fat. Fat is the ideal filler because it is your own tissue and the fat that survives is permanent. Complications are rare in the hands of an experienced surgeon.

The fat is obtained through liposuction of other areas such as the abdomen, flanks (“love-handles”), and back. The body is re-shaped by narrowing the waist and enhancing the buttocks and hips. An “hour-glass” appearance can be reached if done properly.

The fat that is transferred survives in its new location by having small blood vessels grow into the fat cells. Not all of the fat survives. Good fat survival rates range between 50% and 70%. Fat survival depends on many factors including the surgeon’s technique and the post-surgery care (e.g. avoiding sitting).
