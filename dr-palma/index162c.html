What procedure helps correct nose asymmetry?
Nose asymmetry
Aug 5, 2021
Nose asymmetry is a very frequent condition encountered in rhinoplasty. For sure it can be corrected but you should expect a residual asymmetry albeit unnoticeable to the “standard” oberver’s eyes. Proper diagnosis and accurate planning is as important as precise, delicate surgery. In my personal experience, endonasal approach provides better result. But this is a matter of personal choice.
Will I be a good candidate for rhinoplasty (for the look I want to achieve)?
ethnic nose
Aug 5, 2021
You have a specific configuration of your tip cartilages called cephalic malposition of lateral crura. Their correction needs advanced tip surgery. There are different technical options, each one with advantages and drawbacks. Thick skin and flimsy cartilages are unfavorable conditions to achieve a defined, sculpted tip.
