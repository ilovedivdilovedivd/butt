The procedure can be repeated unlimited number of times. The effect is retained, on average, six months or a year.

With butt implants, the result is visible at once (It is possible to estimate on the next day after surgery) and persists for many years. After the butt augmentation and final of healing, the scars are practically invisible.

The implant is installed above the muscle,or in the thickness of muscles. If the implant is above the muscle, there is a likelihood that the recovery period will take place more easily, than when you install of the implant in the thickness of muscles. However, this installation is not always possible – if not have enough of its own covering tissues, the implant can be noticeable.

The recovery period

The recovery period is generally lasts for about four months. Unpleasant sensations in the area of the buttocks disappear as soon as the healing process occurs, and a seam becomes invisible.

Buttocks – is part of the body, which is actively used in daily life. Furthermore, during surgery , the muscular layers are affected. Therefore recovery is associated with some additional inconvenience. Patients are advised to sit only at a certain angle.

During the first month after surgery in Alabama, all the doctor’s recommendations must be observed strictly:

  • wearing compression underwear;
  • intake of analgesics and other medications to relieve muscle tone;

Patients are allowed to resume employment by fitness, after 2 months of surgery.

Contraindications and potential complications

The main contraindications for AL brazilian butt lift:

  1. systemic diseases of internal organs (in the acute stage)
  2. coagulation disorders,
  3. diabetes,
  4. cardiovascular disease and other possible dysfunctions of vital of systems (of the cardiovascular, of the circulatory, endocrine).

Possible complications after the operation: a hematoma, the development of the inflammatory process, the divergence of seams, as well as capsular contracture – the formation of compaction tissue around the implant. The probability of developing complications (subject to all recommendations of the doctor in the postoperative period) is minimal and amounts to no more than a few percent.
