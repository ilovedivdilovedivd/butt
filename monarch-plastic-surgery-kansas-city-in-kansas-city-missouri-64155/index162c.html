Keith Hodge, MD

Name: Keith Hodge, MD
Statement: Dr. Keith Hodge is a board-certified plastic surgeon practicing in Leawood, Kansas. Highly skilled in all procedures relating to the aesthetic improvement of the body, he specializes in mommy makeovers, breast enhancement, and tummy tucks.

New to the Monarch Plastic Surgery practice, patients can have the ground-breaking experience of virtually trying on Sientra implants.

At his practice, Monarch Plastic Surgery, he is known for his expertise in a wide range of surgical procedures, from the cosmetic to the reconstructive. Alongside breast augmentations, facelifts, and other body contouring procedures, he also performs breast reconstruction surgery, lesion removal, and hand surgery for patients dealing with degenerative conditions, injuries, or birth defects relating to the hands.

Plastic surgery is Dr. Hodge’s passion. He takes pride in helping people reach their goals so they can regain their self-confidence and healthy self-esteem. He knows how important it is to develop and maintain a relationship with his patients. Providing care that is safe and compassionate, with quality that surpasses the patient’s expectations, are his ultimate goals.

Before embarking on his medical career, Dr. Hodge attended Texas A&M University. He then earned his medical degree from the University of Texas Health Science Center at San Antonio, which was followed by general surgery training at the University of Arkansas for Medical Sciences. Dr. Hodge then attended the Connecticut Combined Hand Surgery Service for his hand surgery training before completing his plastic surgery residency at the University of Kansas Medical Center.

Board-certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery, Dr. Hodge also holds membership with the American Society of Aesthetic Plastic Surgery and the American Society of Plastic Surgery. He is also a fellow of the American College of Surgeons and has been named a RealSelf Top Doctor. He also served as president of the Kansas City Plastic Surgery Society and held a role as clinical assistant professor at the University of Kansas Department of Plastic Surgery.
