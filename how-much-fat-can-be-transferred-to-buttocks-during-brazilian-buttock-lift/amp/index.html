It’s not unusual for patients to be concerned about how much fat can be transferred when they undergo a Brazilian butt lift. The answer to this question depends upon a large number of variables that occur pre-operatively, post-operatively and intra-operatively. These variables include the availability of donor sites, the quality of the recipient site, the technique utilized to process the fat, the patient’s aesthetic goals and the post-operative management.

Without pictures or a physical examination it’s virtually impossible to estimate how much fat can be transferred in any particular patient. In most patients 400cc’s per buttock would be considered a reasonable number, but it’s not unusual for significantly larger amounts of fat to be grafted. If you’re considering Brazilian butt lift surgery it’s important that you consult a board certified plastic surgeon. This surgeon should be able to estimate the amount of fat that will be transferred based on your unique anatomic findings and aesthetic goals. (Richard J. Bruneteau, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)

How much fat can be transferred during a BBL

The amount of fat that can be transferred will depend on the amount of the fat that can be harvested and on the pliability of the recipient site in order to accommodate the amount transferred. Saying this, usually in the buttock area we can inject somewhere between 300 cc to 600 cc per side depending on the shape we need to achieve and the eleasticity of the tissues that will accommodate the fat injected. In few cases the volume exceeds the 600 cc per side but this will be individualized and without a personal physical examination it will be very difficult to give a sure answer. (Shady Hayek, MD, Lebanon Plastic Surgeon)
