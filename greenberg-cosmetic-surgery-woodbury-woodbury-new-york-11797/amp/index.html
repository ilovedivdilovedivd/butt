Stephen T. Greenberg, MD, FACS

Name: Stephen T. Greenberg, MD, FACS
Statement: Dr. Stephen T. Greenberg is a double board-certified plastic surgeon and founder and medical director of Greenberg Cosmetic Surgery and Dermatology, with offices strategically located in Manhattan, Southampton, Woodbury, Smithtown, New York, and Boca Raton, Florida. His practices are distinguished by some of the most advanced, fully-accredited ambulatory surgical centers in the United States.

Dr. Greenberg specializes in a wide array of procedures, including mini-incision rapid recovery breast augmentation, breast lifts, liposuction, tummy tucks, and mommy makeovers. His expertise also encompasses rhinoplasty, eyelid surgery, facelifts, and various other cosmetic enhancements. Patients deeply appreciate his warm bedside manner and the informative consultations he provides.

Dr. Greenberg holds a bachelor's degree and medical degree with honors from George Washington University in Washington, D.C. Following a residency in general surgery at the renowned New York Hospital-Cornell University Medical Center, he further honed his skills with a residency in plastic and reconstructive surgery at Philadelphia's Hospital of the University of Pennsylvania. He is duly board-certified by both the American Board of Surgery and the American Board of Plastic Surgery.

Beyond his dedication to patient care, Dr. Greenberg actively participates in multiple professional organizations, including the American Medical Association, the Nassau County Medical Society, the American Society of Plastic Surgeons, and the New York Regional Society of Plastic Surgeons.

Notably, Dr. Greenberg is recognized for his philanthropic endeavors. He has organized fundraising events for notable organizations such as Dress for Success of Nassau County, the Make-A-Wish Foundation of Metropolitan New York, and the Carol M. Baldwin Breast Cancer Research Foundation, among others. His generosity has earned him honors from Dress for Success, and he was named Plastic Surgeon of the Year at the Miracle Maker's Ball in 2004.
