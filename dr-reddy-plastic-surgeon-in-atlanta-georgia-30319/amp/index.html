Will this weird pulling/stretch in my lower tummy go away over time?
Pulling after TT
Nov 4, 2018
The pulling in the lower abdomen will improve over tome as the healing tissue remodels.  Wound remodeling is a normal part of the healing process and occurs in the direction of applied tension.  It may take up to 1-year for a wound to remodel.

I am getting a tummy tuck. Will a massage help with my drains?
TT and massages
Oct 24, 2018
The bane of TT is addressing the fluids post-surgery and this is managed by the drains.  Immediate post-operative massages are not recommended as they may exacerbate fluid or seroma.  I do recommend Lymphedema massages for persistent swelling or soft tissue mobilization about 6-weeks after. This helps with any lumpy-bumpy feeling that is normal after any TT.  
Epidural anesthesia and foley catheter in tummy tuck. Is this good practice?
Epidural and Foley with TT
Oct 24, 2018
The reason you would have a Foley with almost any epidural is due to the possibility of urinary retention.This course of treatment strikes me as overkill.  Unless you are unable to safely undergo anesthesia ( breathing problems etc), GA is safe and does not significantly increase risk.  Our goal with TT is early ambulation (walking) after surgery in order to promote rapid recovery.  An epidural would potentially delay this as well.
