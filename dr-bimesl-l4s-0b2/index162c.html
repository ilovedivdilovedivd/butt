How does Dysport in your DAO muscle affect your smile?
Elevate Your Smile With Dysport!
Dec 2, 2022
Hi,DAO stands for Depressor Anguli Oris - this is a "depressor" muscle and if too active, this gives a frowning appearance, pulling the smile downwards. Naturally, over time the activation of this muscle can create deep marionette lines. When Dysport is injected into this muscle it becomes relaxed allowing for your smile to pull upwards instead of downwards. This will soften marionette lines and create an "elevated" smile. This is a normal and expected response to injecting Dysport in this area.
