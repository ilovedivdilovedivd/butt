Basic understanding of surgery

Gluteal region is an important component of a beautiful figure. In addition to these reasons, after 30 years age-related changes have a significant impact on the buttox, which results to the displacement of the gluteal fold, ptosis of tissues, loss of elasticity, appearance of hollows on the side and changing the contour of the buttocks.

Endoprosthesis allows to increase buttox, give them roundness with smooth contours that changes the proportions of the figure as a whole and provides an attractive harmony of its shape.

There are different criteria for determining the ideal size and shape of the buttox, but they are all relative and the selection of implants is performed in accordance with the individual characteristics of the body structure, and with the wishes of patients.

Types and features of buttox implants

According to the structure buttox implants do not differ much from the breast implants and is a shell that is filled with a highly cohesive (viscous) silicone gel a volume of 140-180 ml. It does not flow into the surrounding tissue if shell is damaged and has a high “memory effect.” The main difference – it is the appropriate thickness (up to 1000 microns). It provides greater strength (in comparison with breast endoprostheses) due with susceptibility to significant loads. Buttocks with such implants look natural in appearance and to the touch.

Implants of different sizes are available in three forms:

  1. Round, designed to improve the roundness of the buttox. However, they exclude an anatomical change in shape during movement. In addition, their projection on the body is quite high, which in some patients can lead to unnatural contours.
  2. Oval, or anatomical, which are used for the same purpose, but are devoid of the above disadvantages.
  3. Teardrop-shaped, used to correct deformities, formed as a result of injury or disease.

The surface of the capsule of the prosthesis may be smooth or textured. The latter are characterized by high strength and a low probability of the offset.

Technique of buttox implants surgery

Surgery to increase the buttocks can be made after 18 years of any age. It is performed during 1.5-3 hours under general anesthesia or spinal anesthesia. The essence of surgery consists in small (3-5 cm) incision of the skin in the crease between the buttocks in the sacrum. After that formed the box, where are placed silicone implants. Fabrics are sewn on it, and is applied a cosmetic seam to the skin. Depending on the shape of the buttocks, the implant can be established under the gluteus maximus, under the superficial fascia that covers the the muscle or inside the latter. Such variants of installation of the prosthesis is lowered to 3-4% the subsequent possibility of formation contracture and fibrous capsule, in comparison with subcutaneous placement.

Despite its apparent simplicity, buttox implants requires professionalism of the surgeon due to the necessity of reliable fixation of the prosthesis in order to prevent its displacement, with the presence of a plurality of blood vessels, nerve branches, damage of which could lead to serious complications.

The main complications of surgery

Despite their rarity possible cases:

  • occurrence of the asymmetry of the gluteal region;
  • implant displacement and output from contours beyond its box;
  • pain in the leg because of the displacement of the implant and its pressure on the sciatic nerve;
  • changes in gait as a result of damage of the sciatic nerve during surgery or irritation it by formed scars;
  • accumulation of interstitial fluid in the wound (seroma), which is removed by puncture.

Indications and contraindications for surgery

Aesthetic buttox implants are indicated in the following cases:

  1. The patient’s desire to change the aesthetic shape and size of the buttocks.
  2. Insufficient development of the gluteal muscles, which are not amenable to correction by non-surgical methods.
  3. Ptosis of soft tissues.
  4. Muscle asymmetry.
  5. Dystrophic changes or violation of the proportions of shape as a result of injury or disease.

The main contraindications for buttox implants:

  • Severe chronic systemic diseases, especially renal and hepatic failure.
  • Blood clotting disorders.
  • Some endocrine diseases, especially diabetes.
  • Acute infectious diseases.
  • Excess body weight.

Features of the postoperative period

In the hospital after surgery patient is placed for 1-2 days. Discomfort persists for 2 weeks to 1 month and disappears after the elimination of the main tissue edema and their complete healing. It is recommended wearing compression underwear for 2-3 weeks, the choice of position on a chair in order to avoid significant pressure on the implants, the rejection of physical activity. After 1.5 months are permitted exercises without any loading restrictions.

The result of buttox implants is determined after 3-4 months after performing, when the swelling disappears completely. In the future with occurrence of diseases requiring an intramuscular injection of drugs, they must be implemented in other areas of the body.
